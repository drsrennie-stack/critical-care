Your intake, your starting point.

Before the program begins, we need to know where you are. Not where you hope to be, not where you think you should be. Where you actually are right now.

What this intake is

This intake does three things. It helps us assign you to a team with good balance. It gives you a personalized baseline report at the end, so you leave with something useful. And it is the first act of honest self-assessment this program will ask of you. Everything here is private between you and your facilitator.

How to answer

Be truthful. No one is scoring what you report. The more accurate your answers, the more useful the program will be. If a question feels hard, that's often a signal the honest answer is the important one. If you need to skip something, skip it, but aim for honesty over comfort.

What to expect

Time: About 20 to 25 minutes. You can save and come back later.

Sections: 9 sections covering your current situation, study habits, learning profile, and honest reflection.

Result: A personalized baseline report you'll review in week 1.

Your next 8 weeks

The program runs on a weekly STAT cycle. Each week you'll:

Status — a short written audit of your week, submitted before the live session. SOAP-structured self-assessment.

Triage — a team case discussion during the live session. You reason about a composite student who is in a situation like yours, with your team.

Action — a personal recovery plan for the coming week, written right after the team discussion. Specific, time-bounded, realistic.

Track — at the start of next week's Status, you report whether you did what you said you would do. This is how accountability becomes structural rather than aspirational.
