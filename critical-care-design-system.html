02

Contrast and compliance

WCAG AAA is the target for body text (7:1 contrast) and at minimum AA (4.5:1) for decorative and large-text elements. This table documents the specific combinations and their compliance levels.

Sample Combination Ratio Normal Large
Body text Charcoal on white 13.15:1 AAA AAA
Secondary Slate on white 10.31:1 AAA AAA
Muted Muted slate on white 7.56:1 AAA AAA
Navy heading Navy on white 15.30:1 AAA AAA
Deep gold Deep gold on white 5.22:1 AA AAA
Brushed gold Brushed gold on white 3.54:1 Fail AA
Navy surface Warm white on navy 17.80:1 AAA AAA
Gold on navy Bright gold on navy 7.88:1 AAA AAA
Bright gold button Navy on bright gold 6.83:1 AA AAA

Gold usage rule

Brushed gold (#B8904D) is for large-text, icons, rules, and borders where AA is sufficient. For any gold text at normal body size, use deep gold (#8F6E38) to maintain AAA compliance. On navy surfaces, bright gold (#D4A962) is AAA-compliant at all sizes. Bright gold on white ONLY works with navy text (6.83:1), never with white text (fails contrast).

Quick reference: which gold where?

Body text on white: Deep gold (#8F6E38) only.
Headings and large text on white: Brushed gold (#B8904D) or deep gold.
Rules, borders, icons, decorative elements: Brushed gold (#B8904D).
Text on navy: Bright gold (#D4A962).
Button with white text: Deep gold (#8F6E38) background.
Button with navy text: Bright gold (#D4A962) background. This is the brightest, most eye-catching button in the system.

07

Disclosures and accordions

Progressive disclosure keeps interfaces uncluttered and lets students pull information forward when they need it.

The status audit is a weekly check-in, submitted before your team session. It has four sections: execution check (what you did last week), current vitals (your grade and upcoming work), a targeted diagnostic specific to this week's theme, and a trust check about what you are ready to share with your team. It takes about 15 minutes honestly.

Life happens. The program has a built-in re-entry clause that allows one structured return after a missed week, including a brief conversation about what happened and what needs to change. Missing multiple weeks without re-engagement is a signal the program is not working for you, and we address that directly rather than letting it drift.

08

Tabs

Tab navigation for grouped content. Gold underline marks the active tab.

Status: individual audit

Students complete before class. Honest reporting on the previous week's commitments, current vitals, and a targeted diagnostic. Scored for completion, not performance.

Rounds: team case discussion

Teams work a common case, diagnose what's going wrong for the student in the case, and build a recovery plan. Facilitated discussion produces the reasoning frameworks students will apply to themselves.

Orders: personal application

Students take the team's reasoning and apply it to their own situation. Write a 7-day plan. Commit to a 24-hour action. Identify the study method most likely to be wrong.

Progress Note: next-cycle accountability

Next week's status audit opens with execution check on this week's orders. The loop closes. No one escapes without reporting back.

14

Accessibility notes

This system targets WCAG AAA where reasonable and AA at minimum. Specific commitments and requirements for builders.

Contrast

Text ratios

Body text targets 7:1 (AAA). Large text and UI elements target 4.5:1 (AA) minimum. All color combinations documented in section 02.

Focus

Visible focus rings

All interactive elements have a 3px gold focus ring with a white inner offset for visibility on any background. Focus ring contrast exceeds 3:1 against surrounding content.

Touch targets

Minimum 44px

All buttons and controls meet the 44x44px minimum touch target. Form inputs meet or exceed 48px height for comfortable tap interaction.

Motion

Reduced motion support

All animations and transitions are disabled for users with prefers-reduced-motion. No autoplay, no parallax, no vestibular triggers.

Semantics

Proper HTML structure

Heading hierarchy without skips. Form labels always associated with inputs. ARIA used only where semantic HTML is insufficient. Skip links on every page.

Keyboard

Full keyboard navigation

Every interactive element reachable and operable by keyboard. Tab order follows visual order. Disclosure and tab components follow ARIA authoring practices.

15

Print optimization

Many program documents are printed by students. A dedicated print stylesheet conserves ink and improves physical document quality.

Print style behaviors

Shadows convert to borders. Interactive-only elements (buttons, tabs) are hidden. Feature blocks print as outlined containers rather than navy fills. Gold elements print as readable deep gold. Page breaks avoided within cards and after headings.

Try printing this page (or using browser print preview) to see the optimization in practice.
